# GEO Paper Outline - Pre-Writing Plan

## Title Options
1. "Your Website Is Invisible to AI. Here Is How to Fix It."
2. "Generative Engine Optimization: Why the Brands That Adapt First Will Win the Next Decade of Search"
3. "The End of Google as We Know It: A Practical Framework for AI-First Visibility"

## Structure: Problem-Cause-Solution with Monroe's Motivated Sequence

### 1. ATTENTION (300 words)
- Open with a scenario: a consumer asks ChatGPT "best leather wallet under $100" and your brand doesn't appear
- Hit with the Gartner stat: search volume dropping 25% by 2026
- SparkToro data: 58.5% of Google searches already produce zero clicks
- The question: if traditional search is eroding, where is discovery happening?

### 2. NEED / THE SHIFT (400 words)
- Define the shift: from search engines to answer engines
- Perplexity at 100M+ monthly queries, ChatGPT search, Google AI Overviews (1B+ users)
- The fundamental difference: AI doesn't rank pages, it synthesizes answers and cites sources
- What this means: if your content isn't being cited, you don't exist in the new discovery layer

### 3. SATISFACTION / THE FRAMEWORK (800 words)
- Introduce GEO formally, cite the Georgia Tech/Princeton paper
- The 9 strategies tested and their results:
  - Quotation Addition: +41% visibility (best)
  - Statistics Addition: +33%
  - Cite Sources: +28%
  - Keyword Stuffing: -10% (actively harmful)
- Key insight: what works for Google HURTS you in AI
- Domain-specific findings (different strategies for different content types)
- The democratization angle: lower-ranked sites gain 95-115%, leveling the playing field

### 4. VISUALIZATION / PRACTICAL FRAMEWORK (500 words)
- My proposed GEO framework for brands:
  1. Audit your AI visibility (search your brand in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude)
  2. Structure content for citation (stats, quotes, clear claims)
  3. Build topical authority through depth, not breadth
  4. Optimize for RAG retrieval (structured data, clear headings, FAQ schema)
  5. Monitor and iterate (emerging tools: Otterly.ai, etc.)
- Real examples of what optimized vs unoptimized content looks like

### 5. ACTION / THE URGENCY (300 words)
- First-mover advantage: AI training data and retrieval indices are being built NOW
- The brands that establish authority in AI responses today will compound that advantage
- Close with a forward-looking statement about the next 3-5 years

## Writing Voice Rules
- First person singular where it adds authority ("In my experience managing content across 10+ channels...")
- Vary sentence length aggressively (5 words. Then a 30-word sentence that builds.)
- Use parenthetical asides (the kind that feel like thinking out loud)
- Include one self-correction ("Actually, that number is more nuanced than it sounds.")
- Reference specific tools by name, not generic categories
- Canadian spelling where applicable
- No em dashes
- Cite with inline references, not footnotes
