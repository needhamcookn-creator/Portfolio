Artimemo: Social Content + Paid Ad Campaign

Client: Artimemo (@artimemo0) | DTC Decorative Stationery | TikTok + Instagram
The Brief
Artimemo makes handcrafted illuminated calendars and 3D memo pads with intricate architectural designs. Price point: $38-$80. The founder (Yifeng Zhu) needed to scale from $3K/month to profitability through social content and paid ads on TikTok and Instagram.
The Challenge
The products are gorgeous but hard to explain in a feed. A "3D paper calendar that lights up" sounds like a novelty toy. The copy needed to reposition these as premium desk art that people would actually want to display, not a gimmick they would regret buying.

My Approach

I stopped trying to describe the product and started describing the moment. Nobody buys a $54 memo pad because of the paper quality. They buy it because they picture it sitting on their desk and someone asking about it. That became the angle for everything: the reaction, not the product.

Landing Page Copy

YouTube Mentorship Program: Landing Page

Own Project | Conversion Page for $1,699 Mentorship | 885+ Community Members
The Brief
I run a 90-day mentorship program teaching people how to build faceless YouTube channels using AI tools. I needed a landing page that converts Discord community members into paid applicants without using the high-pressure guru playbook that dominates this space.
The Challenge
Every YouTube mentor sells with fake urgency and income screenshots. My audience has been burned before. The page needed to sell a $1,699 program to people who are actively suspicious of anyone selling a $1,699 program.

My Approach

I wrote the page like I was talking to someone who had already been scammed. Every section preemptively addresses the objection they are thinking but would never type in the comments. The entire structure is: acknowledge skepticism, show proof without bragging, explain exactly what they get, and make the application itself the filter.

The Copy

Hero Section

Build a YouTube channel that pays your rent. Without showing your face.

90 days. A system that works. A mentor who actually does this for a living.

Opening Section

I run a portfolio of faceless YouTube channels that generates over $50,000 a month. Not from courses. Not from coaching. From ad revenue on videos I create using AI tools and workflows I built from scratch.

I am not telling you that to impress you. I am telling you because the person teaching you should be doing the thing they are teaching. Most people in this space are not. They made their money selling the dream, not living it.

This mentorship is different because I do not need your money. The channels pay my bills. I teach because I genuinely like watching people figure this out.

"What This Is Not" Section

I want to be upfront about what you are not getting:

  • This is not a course. There are no pre-recorded modules you watch alone at 2am feeling motivated but not actually doing anything.
  • This is not a community with no leadership. I show up. Every week. On camera. Screen-sharing my actual workflow, not slides.
  • This is not a get-rich-quick pitch. Some people in the program are earning within 60 days. Some take longer. The ones who do the work get results. The ones who want a magic button do not.
CTA Section

Applications are reviewed individually. I accept people based on commitment, not budget. If you cannot afford it right now, that is fine. The free Discord is not going anywhere and neither am I.

But if you are ready to stop watching tutorials and start building something, fill out the application. It takes about 5 minutes. I read every single one.

Apply for the Next Cohort

Why It Worked

The page converts at roughly 8% from visitor to application. The key insight was that anti-sell copy actually sells better to a skeptical audience. Every line that says "I do not need your money" or "the free Discord is not going anywhere" builds the trust that makes people feel safe enough to spend $1,699. The application filter also creates perceived exclusivity without fake scarcity.

~8% visitor-to-application rate 885+ community members in free Discord $1,699 price point with money-back guarantee

Artimemo: Product Launch Email Sequence

Client: Artimemo | Shopify + Klaviyo | 3-Email Launch Flow
The Brief
Artimemo was launching a new illuminated calendar collection (4 new designs). Yifeng had an email list of ~8,000 from previous buyers and website visitors but had never sent a proper launch sequence. He wanted to drive pre-orders before the TikTok campaign went live.
The Challenge
The list was cold. Many subscribers had not opened an email in months. The first email needed to re-engage without feeling like spam, and the sequence needed to build enough urgency to drive purchases of a $54-$80 product from people who had never bought before.

The Work

Email 1 - Teaser (sent 5 days before launch)
Subject: We have been working on something for 4 months

Honest question: do you remember signing up for this list?

If not, totally fair. We have been quiet. Really quiet. But it was not because we forgot about you. It was because we were heads-down building something new and did not want to send filler emails pretending to have news when we did not.

We have news now.

Four new designs. Our best work yet. They ship in 5 days. And the people on this list get to see them before anyone on TikTok or Instagram does.

I will send photos on Wednesday. For now, just know it was worth the wait.

Yifeng

Email 2 - Reveal (sent 2 days before launch)
Subject: Here they are (4 new designs)

I told you Wednesday. It is Wednesday.

[Image grid: 4 new calendar designs - Santorini, Earth, Panda, Mushroom House]

Every one is hand-cut paper. Every one lights up from the inside. And every one took our design team between 6 and 10 weeks to develop from sketch to finished product.

My personal favorite is the Earth one. There is something about watching it glow at night on my desk that makes the late nights feel a little less heavy.

These go live on the site Friday at 9am EST. If you want one, I would not wait too long. Our last launch sold through the first batch in 4 days.

I am not saying that to pressure you. I am saying it because I got a lot of emails last time from people who waited and missed out, and I would rather give you a heads up than apologize later.

See you Friday.

Yifeng

Email 3 - Launch Day
Subject: They are live

9am. All four new calendars are on the site.

If you have been waiting, this is it. Free shipping on every order. No code needed.

I will keep this short because you do not need a sales pitch. You have seen the photos. You either want one or you do not. Either way, I appreciate you being here.

Shop the New Collection

Yifeng

Why It Worked

Email 1 re-engaged a cold list by being honest about the silence instead of pretending it did not happen. That "do you remember signing up?" opening had a 44% open rate because it felt human, not automated. The sequence builds anticipation across 5 days without feeling like a countdown timer. Email 3 is deliberately short because by launch day the selling is done. The people who open that email are ready. They just need the link.

44% open rate on Email 1 (cold list) First batch sold out in 4 days Part of the $3K to $40K/month growth

Artimemo: Shopify Product Page Rewrite

Client: Artimemo | Shopify Storefront | Product Page Copy
The Brief
Artimemo's Shopify product pages were converting at 1.8%. The descriptions read like spec sheets: dimensions, materials, weight. Nothing about why someone would actually want one. Yifeng asked me to rewrite the product copy to match the voice we built on social.
The Challenge
Product pages need to do two things at once: give the practical buyer the specs they need (size, materials, shipping) and give the emotional buyer the feeling they need. Most Shopify pages only do one. The rewrite needed to do both without making the page longer.

Before (Original Copy)

Sakura Tree House Calendar

3D Illuminated Calendar with LED light. Made from high-quality paper. Beautiful cherry blossom design. Perfect for desk decoration. Dimensions: 7.5 x 3.5 x 6 inches. Includes USB-powered LED light. Makes a great gift.

After (My Rewrite)

Sakura Tree House Calendar

The calendar people ask about on your Zoom calls.

Every petal is hand-cut from paper. The light inside shifts the mood depending on the time of day. Warm in the morning. Soft and cinematic at night. It turns whatever surface you put it on into something worth looking at.

People will ask if it is real. It is. People will ask where you got it. You are welcome.

Hand-cut paper construction. USB-powered LED. 7.5 x 3.5 x 6 inches. Ships free in 5-7 business days. 30-day returns.

Why It Worked

The original copy listed features. The rewrite leads with the experience and puts specs at the bottom where they belong. The opening line ("the calendar people ask about on your Zoom calls") immediately creates a scenario the reader can picture. The specs are still there for the practical buyer, just moved below the fold so they do not kill the energy.

1.8% to 2.4% product page conversion rate Same traffic, 33% more revenue per visitor
Long-Form Scripts

YouTube Channel Network: Three Distinct Voices

Own Channels | 10+ Properties | Finance, Geopolitics, Education
The Brief
I write every script for a portfolio of YouTube channels across completely different niches. Each channel has its own voice, audience, and tone. The same person writing a tax education video for retirees also writes geopolitical analysis and personal development content for college students. Adapting voice across audiences is the core skill.
The Challenge
A 65-year-old retiree and a 22-year-old college student do not respond to the same writing. The tax channel needs calm authority and insider knowledge. The geopolitics channel needs analytical intensity and historical depth. The education channel needs casual confidence without sounding preachy. Same writer, three completely different people on the other end.

Channel 1: Markus Graves (Tax & Finance Education)

Voice: Calm authority. Trusted accountant who knows things the IRS does not want you to know. Audience: Retirees 55-75+.

"What the IRS Is Quietly Doing Right Now Should Be Front Page News" (15 min)

AI systems now have the ability to see every deposit, every withdrawal, and every transaction you make. And the IRS just gave itself the power to drain your bank account to zero without ever stepping into a courtroom.

87,000 new agents. $80 billion in new funding. And none of it made front page news.

In this video: the four things the IRS quietly changed, how their new AI tracks your money in real time, the 1983 rule that is now catching millions of Americans who did nothing wrong, and five things you need to do right now to protect yourself.

"The $250,000 Tax Rule Is Changing" (25 min)

If you own a home and you are over 60, the tax rules just shifted underneath you. And the IRS did not send you a warning.

The $250,000 home sale exclusion that millions of Americans rely on has not changed. But four things around it did. And together, they can cost you over $75,000 if you do not understand how they interact.

There is a trap inside this rule that cost one homeowner over $75,000 in federal taxes on the sale of a home they owned for nearly 30 years. Not because they cheated. Because of a technicality that the IRS never warned them about.

Channel 2: Interstellar Eye (Geopolitical Analysis)

Voice: Academic intensity. Professor delivering analysis the mainstream media will not. Audience: News-aware adults 25-55.

"America Is Negotiating With Bombs. This Is How It Ends." (18 min)

America's own Defense Secretary just admitted something that should terrify everyone watching. On March 26th, Pete Hegseth looked into the camera and said, quote, "The Department of War will continue negotiating with bombs."

Negotiating with bombs. Like dropping explosives on 87 million people is just another form of diplomacy. Here is what nobody in that room mentioned. This strategy has been tried five times in the last 60 years. It failed every single time. Not sometimes. Not mostly. Every time.

Today I am going to show you the five cases, the game theory behind why bombing never produces the deal the bomber wants, and exactly where this leads based on 60 years of data.

"Here's Who Is Going to Win and Lose This War" (20 min)

Every war produces winners and losers. That is not a political statement. It is a mathematical certainty. Resources flow from one place to another. Alliances shift. Power vacuums open and someone fills them.

Eight predictions were made before this war started. Eight have been confirmed. An 86 percent accuracy rate across 111 tracked predictions. Today I am going country by country and telling you exactly who wins, who loses, and who comes out the other side in a position they have never held before.

Channel 3: NickNc (Personal Development)

Voice: Casual, vulnerable, philosophical. Not a guru. A peer who happens to be thinking out loud. Audience: College-age creators 18-25.

"AI Is Replacing YouTube Creators. I Know Because I Am The One Replacing Them." (12 min)

Whatever business or project you are working on is probably going to fail. So just start already.

I am not saying that to be negative. I am saying it because the biggest lie in this space is that you need the perfect idea, the perfect plan, and the perfect execution before you are allowed to begin. You do not. You need to start messy, learn fast, and iterate.

Every channel I run today started with a terrible first video. Every single one. The difference between me and the people still planning is that I posted the terrible video and kept going.

Writing Process

Every script follows a structure I developed through testing: the curiosity gap opens within 7 seconds. New information lands every 30-60 seconds. Transitions use "but" and "therefore" instead of "next" or "also." The main payoff resolves around the 75% mark, and the last 25% delivers something the viewer was not expecting.

The voice switches completely between channels. Markus Graves never says "I." Interstellar Eye never uses casual language. NickNc never sounds like an authority figure. Same writer, three completely different readers. That flexibility is the skill.

50%+ average view duration on 15-25 min videos $50K+/month portfolio ad revenue 12+ scripts written per month
Short-Form Scripts (Personal Brand)

NickNc Personal Brand: Miami Philosophy Series

Own Brand | TikTok + Instagram Reels | Short-Form (45-55s each)
The Brief
I wrote a 12-part short-form philosophy series for my personal brand launch. Shot in Miami. Each video is 45-55 seconds, filmed on a high-rise balcony with warm lighting. No sales pitch. No CTA. Just one idea, delivered with conviction, designed to make someone stop scrolling and sit with something uncomfortable for a minute.
The Challenge
The creator space is drowning in "5 tips to grow your channel" content. I needed to stand out by going the opposite direction: vulnerability, philosophy, and ideas that stick in your head at 2am. The copy had to sound like a real person thinking out loud, not a script being read.

Selected Scripts

"You flinch at your own excitement." (55s)

You know what scares you more than failure? Getting excited again.

You have had the idea before. The rush hits. You can see the whole thing playing out. And then something in the back of your brain whispers, "here we go again."

So you kill it. Not because you are lazy. Because the last time you let yourself believe something would work, it didn't. And the fall from hope hurt worse than the fall from nothing.

That flinch? That is not wisdom. That is scar tissue pretending to be strategy.

You are not afraid of failure. Failure you can handle. You have handled it before. You are afraid of hope. Because hope has hurt you worse than failure ever did.

Stop flinching. The next one might be the one that works. And you will never know if you keep pulling back the second it starts to feel real.

"You're not behind." (50s)

You have this clock in your head that says you should have figured it out by now. And you did not set that clock. The internet set it.

Some kid posted his revenue screenshot and suddenly your timeline feels broken. You are comparing your Chapter 3 to someone else's Chapter 12 and wondering what went wrong.

Nothing went wrong. You are not behind. There is no race. The person you are comparing yourself to is also comparing themselves to someone else. It goes all the way up.

I am eighteen. I have made more money than most adults I know. And some days I still feel behind. That should tell you everything you need to know about the feeling.

The window is not closing. There was never a window. That was a marketing tactic.

"I know what 2am feels like." (45s)

Nobody talks about the 2am version of success.

The version where your phone is off. The content is posted. The numbers look good. And you are staring at the ceiling wondering why it still does not feel like enough.

I am eighteen. I am financially free. And I have laid in that exact silence more times than I will ever admit on camera.

The money does not make that feeling stop. The followers do not make that feeling stop. The only thing that makes it stop is being honest about the fact that it exists.

So here it is. I do not have it figured out. Nobody does. The people who say they do are selling you something.

Why This Works

Each script follows the same structure: open with a feeling the viewer has but cannot articulate, escalate the tension by naming what they are actually afraid of, then land on one line they will remember. No tips. No frameworks. Just emotional precision. The series generated 800K+ views in the first week and converted viewers into an 885-member Discord community without a single CTA in any video. The content was the funnel.

800K+ views in first week 885+ Discord members from organic only 12 scripts in the series
Corporate Copywriting

ARCA: X-Prize Application Video Script

Client: ARCA (Carbon Removal Technology) | Corporate Video | Stakeholder Audience
The Brief
ARCA, a carbon removal technology company, needed a professional video for their X-Prize award application. The audience was a panel of judges evaluating hundreds of submissions. The video had to communicate complex carbon capture technology in under 5 minutes while positioning ARCA as a credible, investable operation.
The Challenge
Carbon removal is dense science. The judges are smart but reviewing dozens of applications per day. The script needed to be technically accurate without being technical. It needed to make the reviewer feel something, not just understand something. Five minutes, one chance.

My Role

I wrote the full script, managed the production workflow (scripting through final edit), and focused the narrative on a single through-line: ARCA does not just capture carbon, they make it economically viable to do so at scale. The technical details supported that story instead of replacing it.

Approach

The script opened by acknowledging the skepticism the judges already feel instead of pretending it does not exist. Every section followed the same pattern: name the hard question, then answer it with data. No hand-waving. No jargon for the sake of sounding smart. The goal was to make a panel of judges who had been reading applications all day actually lean forward and pay attention.

The full script is confidential as part of ARCA's X-Prize application, but I am happy to discuss the project and my process in more detail on a call.

Delivered on deadline for X-Prize submission Broadcast-quality production standard
